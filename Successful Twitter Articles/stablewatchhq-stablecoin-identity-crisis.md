## Core Thesis

Fragmented, outdated identity verification creates an "Identity Tax" that blocks mainstream stablecoin adoption. The solution is an open identity protocol where users control verifiable credentials instead of repeatedly surrendering documents across siloed applications.

## Key Arguments

### The Identity Tax
Every time a user interacts with a new stablecoin application, they start from zero — re-uploading documents, waiting for verification, dropping off. This creates a compounding cost across the ecosystem that the author frames as a "tax" on adoption.

### The Scale of Friction
70% client loss rate during onboarding. 14 screens and 16 fields for a mobile signup. Half of all stablecoin firms spend 5-20% of their budget on compliance. These aren't edge cases — they're the default experience.

### The Solution: Open Identity Protocol
Users hold verifiable credentials that work across applications. Verify once, use everywhere. No more siloed KYC processes that treat every new app as a fresh start.

### The Frontier: Know Your Agent (KYA)
As AI agents begin conducting autonomous transactions, the identity problem extends beyond humans. KYA solutions will need to verify and credential AI agents — a projected $5T market by 2030.

## Why It Works

- **"Identity Tax" is a strong framing device** — Renaming a known problem (KYC friction) as a "tax" makes it feel systemic and urgent rather than just annoying. Taxes are something you want to eliminate.
- **Data density builds authority** — Nearly every claim is backed by a specific number. The piece reads more like a research report than an opinion piece.
- **The 70% stat is a lead-worthy number** — Losing 70% of clients at onboarding is a shocking figure that justifies the entire article's existence.
- **KYA is a forward-looking hook** — Most identity articles stop at humans. Adding the AI agent angle ("Know Your Agent") gives the piece a second thesis that feels novel.
- **Survey data adds primary research credibility** — Including survey findings (73% on KYC pain, 100% on breach prevention) signals original research rather than aggregated opinions.

## Takeaways for Writing

- Naming a problem with a memorable term ("Identity Tax") is more powerful than just describing it — the name becomes the takeaway

- Survey data and original research make an article feel authoritative even if the publication is smaller
- Adding a forward-looking section (KYA / AI agents) gives a familiar topic a novel angle
- Data-heavy reports can work with zero tweet caption — the title and preview do the selling
