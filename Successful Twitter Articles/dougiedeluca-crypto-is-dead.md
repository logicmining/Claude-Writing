## Core Thesis

Crypto as a self-contained industry is dissolving — and that's a good thing. The technology is being absorbed into mainstream infrastructure. The cultural identity of "crypto" has to die for the technology to succeed. "Death is the gateway to something bigger than the industry we've been defending."

## Key Arguments

### The Current State: A Very Liquid MMO
Crypto has been operating as a closed ecosystem — tokens, farming, points programs — essentially a "very liquid MMO" with limited mainstream appeal. The same capital moves between slightly different UIs. Day-one metrics are incredible, month-three retention is disastrous.

### Three Emerging Layers

**Layer 1: Infrastructure**
Blockchains become default rails in specific domains — settlement for payments and markets, cross-border flows where stablecoins are simply better, shared state for identity, collateral, and ownership records. Most users never know or care that it's "on-chain."

**Layer 2: Products**
Apps in fintech, commerce, and beyond use blockchain when it helps, hide complexity aggressively, and compete on standard axes — price, speed, UX, and trust. They market themselves as cheaper, faster, and more global. Not as "on-chain."

**Layer 3: Speculation**
Speculative flows and fee spikes funded the infrastructure that now powers payments. The goal isn't to kill the casino — "it's to stop mistaking the casino for the whole city."

### Winners and Losers
- **Winners:** Teams solving real problems for non-crypto users
- **Losers:** Those dependent solely on farming incentives and crypto-native audiences
- The shift requires abandoning the expectation that the world must become like crypto natives

### The Uncomfortable Truth
"Crypto maturing means crypto, as we've known it, has to die." Success requires accepting that mainstream adoption may not preserve the culture or identity that built the industry.

## Takeaways for Writing

- A provocative title from an insider is far more powerful than from an outsider — the personal stake makes it credible

- Near 1:1 bookmark-to-like ratios indicate a piece that transcends content and becomes reference material
