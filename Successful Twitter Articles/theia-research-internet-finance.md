## Core Thesis

The Internet Financial System (IFS) is a paradigm shift in global financial activity comparable to Gutenberg's printing press. The world will move from less than 10 bps of global financial activity happening on-chain in early 2025 to over 50% within the next two decades.

Opening line: "Our fund thesis is entirely premised on the Internet Financial System. It is our Kierkegaardian idea to live and die for."

## Structure Breakdown

The article follows a **thesis declaration → problem → paradigm shift → five arguments → macro conclusion** structure:

1. **Introduction** — Fund thesis declaration. Sets the stakes immediately ("Kierkegaardian idea to live and die for")
2. **The problem** — Current financial system runs on permissioned, siloed servers that don't allow free capital flow. Local banking oligopolies extract rents.
3. **The paradigm** — Internet Finance is built on permissionless unified servers and smart contract code rather than siloed, permissioned institutions
4. **Five arguments** — Concrete mechanisms for how IFS changes the world
5. **Macro conclusion** — GDP impact and long-term adoption projections

## The Five Arguments

### 1. Cross-Border Capital Flow
Eliminates remittance fees (currently ~7%). Hard-working immigrants paying 7% to send money home is indefensible when it should cost less than $0.01. Reduces extractive net interest margins in emerging economies.

### 2. Improved Property Rights
Enables 5+ billion people in weak property-rights regimes to access stable assets and transparent registries. Self-custody gives people in unstable jurisdictions real ownership.

### 3. Financial Asset Expansion
Democratizes asset issuance — 5M+ tokens launched via Pump.fun since March 2024. Enables novel financial instruments that couldn't exist in traditional finance.

### 4. Operational Efficiency
Smart contracts automate financial functions at radical scale. Key example: "Kamino manages $2.2B with 20 employees." Traditional finance requires orders of magnitude more headcount for equivalent AUM.

### 5. GDP Acceleration
Predicts 75 basis points of additional annual growth globally, adding ~$15 trillion over two decades. Network effects across nearly every dimension of the system compound over time.

## Key Concepts

**The Paradigm Split:** Montealegre splits the crypto world into two paradigms:
- **Fundamentals paradigm** — Believing in the industry's long-term vision, expecting tokens to eventually trade at intrinsic value
- **Periodic mania paradigm** — Treating crypto as a series of speculative cycles

He argues the periodic mania view is wrong and holds the industry back.

**Network Effects:** The simple thesis for why Internet Finance will eventually become critical global financial infrastructure is that it introduces and heightens network effects across nearly every dimension of the system.

## Takeaways for Writing

- When your thesis is ambitious enough, the article title alone can be the hook — no tweet copy needed
- Opening with a philosophical reference ("Kierkegaardian") signals intellectual seriousness and filters for the right audience
- Historical analogies (Gutenberg) work best when they're structural parallels, not hype comparisons

- Splitting your audience's worldview into two paradigms ("fundamentals vs. periodic mania") forces readers to self-identify and engage
